At the 1968 Democratic convention, voters became fed up with the facade of democracy when a candidate who was not even campaigning in the primary won the party nomination.
Democratic PRETENSE
Before 1968, voters were given no control over their party’s nominee. Voters tuned in to the national republican and national democrat conventions to find out who each party had nominated. The few states with primaries held them out of show—they had no effect on who got the party nomination. At the national convention, delegates voted on who would receive the party’s nomination. Except back then, delegates voted based on who the party bosses told them to choose.
